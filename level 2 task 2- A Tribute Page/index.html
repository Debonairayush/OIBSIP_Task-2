Here's a time-line of his Life...

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a prominent leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born in a Hindu merchant caste family in Gujarat's Porbandar state. He grew up to be an Indian activist and leader who took an active part in the Indian Independence movement. He served as an inspiration for various other leaders and the general public who joined the freedom movements led by him.

  • October 2, 1869:Mahatma Gandhi was born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.
  • 1888:travels to London to study law..
  • 1893:travels to South Africa to work as a lawyer. His experiences with racial discrimination and injustice in South Africa become a turning point in his life.
  • 1894: establishes the Natal Indian Congress to fight for the rights of Indians in South Africa.
  • 1915: returns to India and becomes a leader in the Indian independence movement.
  • 1919: The Jallianwala Bagh massacre takes place in Amritsar, India, leading to increased agitation for independence.
  • 1920: launches the Non-Cooperation Movement against British rule, urging Indians to boycott British goods and institutions.
  • 1930:leads the Salt March to protest the British monopoly on salt production.
  • 1932:begins a hunger strike in protest against the British government's treatment of the "untouchables" (Dalits).
  • 1942:launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India.
  • 1947: India gains independence from British rule on August 15. However, the country is partitioned into India and Pakistan, leading to widespread violence and displacement
  • 1948:On January 30, Gandhi is assassinated by Nathuram Godse in New Delhi.
